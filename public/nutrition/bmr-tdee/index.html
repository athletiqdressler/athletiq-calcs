How it works

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) based on sex, age, height and weight. Then it multiplies the BMR by an activity factor to estimate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

How to interpret the results

Your BMR is how many calories your body burns at rest. Your TDEE is your estimated total daily burn, including activity. Eating below your TDEE tends to lead to weight loss; eating slightly above it can support muscle gain when combined with strength training.
