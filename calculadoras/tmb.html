Calorie, BMR & TDEE Calculator

Estimate your basal metabolic rate (BMR) and total daily energy expenditure (TDEE), then see clear calorie targets for fat loss, maintenance and muscle gain — in metric or imperial units.

What these numbers actually mean

BMR is the energy your body uses at complete rest — keeping you alive, not moving, not training. TDEE adjusts that number up based on how much you move and train during the day.

  • BMR: baseline “engine idling” calories.
  • TDEE: BMR × activity factor (your real daily needs).
  • Targets: we apply small surpluses/deficits around TDEE.

How to use this in real life

  • Start around TDEE − 300 to − 500 kcal for sustainable fat loss.
  • Use TDEE + 200 to + 400 kcal for lean muscle gain with hard training.
  • Stay near TDEE when performance and recovery are the priority.

Numbers are a starting point, not a religion. Track body weight, performance and how you feel over 2–3 weeks, then adjust.
