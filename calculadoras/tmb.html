BMR / TMB Calculator

Your basal metabolic rate estimates how many calories your body burns at complete rest. This is the starting point for building a fat-loss deficit or a muscle-gain surplus.

What this calculator actually gives you

• BMR is your “engine idling” — calories burned if you did nothing all day.
• Your real daily expenditure (TDEE) will be higher because of movement, training and NEAT.
• BMR is not a “goal intake”; it's a reference number to plan around.

How to use it in practice

  • For fat loss: usually start around −300 to −500 kcal below your TDEE.
  • For gaining muscle: usually +200 to +400 kcal above your TDEE, with hard training.
  • Extreme deficits below BMR are usually unsustainable and not recommended long term.
